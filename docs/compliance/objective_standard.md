# The Objective Standard — What "Good Enough" Actually Means

The purpose of this document: a reviewer says "your notes aren't good enough." That's a feeling, not a
standard. This is the standard. Every element below traces to an actual FINRA source. When your note
hits every applicable element, "not good enough" has nowhere to stand — and if a reviewer NIGOs it
anyway, you ask them to point to which enumerated element is missing. The burden goes back to them.

Sources (verified):
- FINRA Rule 2330(b) — deferred variable annuity suitability + disclosure
- Reg BI Care Obligation — 17 CFR 240.15l-1(a)(2)(ii)
- FINRA 2024 Annual Regulatory Oversight Report, VA section (the examiner rubric FFS's OSJ applies)
- FFS Reg BI Disclosure Supplement (doc 326349) — FFS's own stated logic

---

## PART 1 — The universal elements (every suitability note, every product)

A suitability note is "complete" under Reg BI's care obligation when it SHOWS (not asserts) each of these.
"Client wants X" is an assertion. "Client, a 49-year-old with $220K income and a 15-year horizon, wants
X because [tie to stated goal]" is showing. The difference is the whole game.

| # | Element | What "showing" looks like | Source |
|---|---------|---------------------------|--------|
| U1 | **Client profile facts** | Age, income, net worth, liquid net worth, time horizon, dependents — the specific numbers, in the note | Reg BI (a)(2)(ii)(A); 2330(b)(2) |
| U2 | **Stated objective** | The client's actual goal in the client's terms (retirement income, LTC protection, legacy) — not a generic "growth" | Reg BI care obligation |
| U3 | **Risk tolerance — ONE value** | A single risk tolerance, consistent across every document in the package | 2024 Report: data integrity |
| U4 | **Basis tied to objective** | WHY this recommendation serves THIS client's stated objective — the causal link spelled out | Reg BI: reasonable basis + customer-specific |
| U5 | **Client understanding** | Evidence the client understands the product's mechanics and risks, not just that they "agreed" | Reg BI care obligation |
| U6 | **Reasonably-available alternatives** | What else was considered, and why it was not chosen — the comparison, briefly | Reg BI (a)(2)(ii)(C) — the most-cited 2024 finding |
| U7 | **Costs & fees disclosed** | The specific cost structure, and that the client was informed of it | 2330(b)(1)(A); Reg BI |
| U8 | **Uniqueness** | Rationale addresses THIS client's specific circumstances; does not replicate another client's note | 2024 Report: named effective practice |

**If a reviewer says "not good enough" and your note contains U1–U8, the response is:**
> "The note documents each element required under Reg BI's care obligation: client profile [U1],
> stated objective [U2], the basis tied to that objective [U4], alternatives considered [U6], and
> cost disclosure [U7]. Please identify the specific element you find insufficient so I can address
> it directly."

That forces the feeling to become a nameable deficiency. They point to a real gap (fix it) or they can't.

## PART 2 — VA / annuity exchange add-ons (Rule 2330 + 2024 examiner rubric)

For any variable annuity exchange, the note must ALSO show these — straight from FINRA's 2024 examiner
checklist. This is the exact list the OSJ is trained on. Miss one and the NIGO is valid.

| # | Element | Source |
|---|---------|--------|
| VA1 | **Loss of existing benefits** — living/death benefit value forfeited, quantified | 2330; 2024 Report finding |
| VA2 | **Surrender charges** — incurred on the old contract, and any NEW surrender period created | 2330(b)(1)(A)(i) |
| VA3 | **Fee/cost comparison** — old vs. new, specific | 2024 Report effective practice |
| VA4 | **36-month prior-exchange check** — has the client exchanged a VA in the last 36 months? | 2330(b)(1)(B) |
| VA5 | **Share-class justification** — why this share class fits the client's horizon and needs | 2024 Report |
| VA6 | **Liquidity consistency** — liquid net worth in the VA consistent with the client's liquidity needs | 2024 Report |
| VA7 | **Source of funds** — where the money is coming from, entered consistently | 2024 Report data list |
| VA8 | **Reasonably-available alternatives to the exchange itself** | Reg BI |

**The Wesner IVA NIGO was VA1 + VA3 (benefits lost / limited protection) not documented specifically.**
It was valid because the note skipped these enumerated elements. With VA1–VA8 present, it doesn't NIGO.

## PART 3 — Buffered / RILA / structured annuity add-ons

For structured/buffer products (the Wesner Athene case), ALSO:

| # | Element | Source |
|---|---------|--------|
| B1 | **Limited protection — stated correctly** | The note says LIMITED downside protection (buffer/floor), NOT full principal protection. FFS 326349 confirms the structure: carrier absorbs first X%, client bears the rest. Say it FFS's own way. |
| B2 | **IVA disclosure — transaction-specific** | If exiting a segment before maturity, the note says an IVA WILL apply to THIS transaction, may be +/-, and could reduce transfer value. Not generic "may be subject to." |
| B3 | **Cap disclosure** | Client understands growth is capped. (326349: "these contracts also have caps.") |
| B4 | **RightBridge consistency** | If IVA applies, the RightBridge MVA/IVA field = Yes. Your note and your report cannot disagree. |

**The Wesner IVA NIGO was B2 + B4.** Both preventable. Both traceable to a rule/standard, not a feeling.

| X2 | **Loan carryover (if loan exists)** — WRITTEN ceding-carrier confirmation the loan carries over; if extinguished it's boot → taxable. Get it before submission. | Hinojosa NIGO; tax law |

## PART 5 — Replacement add-ons (VUL / life)

| # | Element | Source |
|---|---------|--------|
| R1 | **Existing-contract facts VERIFIED** — every claim about what the old contract has/lacks is true. A false differentiator ("the old one didn't have X" when it did) is the single most dangerous line in a replacement note. | 2024 Report: principals must verify existing product values; NAIC #613 |
| R2 | **Replacement notice — signed & dated** by applicant and producer, at/before app | NAIC #613; TX 28 TAC |
| R3 | **Base application face = illustration face** — a rider supplement is not a base application | Hinojosa NIGO |
| R4 | **Benefits lost in the replacement** — quantified | 2330; NAIC #613 |

**The Wesner Brighthouse NIGO was R1** — you stated the old contract lacked index-linked strategies; it
had them. That's not a feeling. That's a false statement in a replacement note the reviewer is REQUIRED
to verify. Valid, and the one that carries real risk.

## How this document is used (three ways)

**1. Write to it.** Before submitting, walk the note against every applicable element. Every box hit =
a note pre-armored against a subjective reviewer, because it visibly satisfies the written standard.

**2. Bounce a NIGO against it.** When "not good enough" lands: which element are they implying?
- Maps to a real element you MISSED → valid, fix it.
- Maps to an element you HIT → respond "the note documents [element]; please identify the specific
  insufficiency," forcing the feeling to become a nameable gap.
- Maps to NO element in this list → they're inventing a requirement. Ask them to cite the rule.

**3. Log the pattern.** Every NIGO that maps to an element you already hit = a data point:
"reviewer NIGO'd a note that met the FINRA standard." Across 150 agents, that's the evidence base.

---

## The honest boundary

This standard makes your notes objectively defensible. It cannot make a subjective reviewer consistent
or fair — no tool can. What it does: it removes the reviewer's ability to hide a feeling behind a
standard, because now there IS a standard, it's written down, it's FINRA's, and your note meets it.
"Not good enough" only survives where there's no defined "good enough." This defines it.
