⚠ Critical correction to your GDC engine — the commission grid is tiered, not flat 80%
The Consolidated Commission Rates document reveals the actual FFS payout structure. The FSA payout rate is NOT a flat 80%. It operates on a rolling 12-month GDC tier system:

Tier 1: Under $14,999 GDC → 40% payout (not 80%)
Tier 2: $15,000–$54,999 GDC → 60% payout
Tier 3: $55,000+ GDC → 80% payout

Your GDC Tracker and commission engine currently calculate all payouts at 80%. This must be corrected immediately. The GDC tracker needs a tier selector or rolling 12-month total input so it applies the correct payout rate per your current tier. At Tier 1, your FSA payout is less than half of what the tracker currently shows.

Fact-find form
Customer Profile Worksheet
31-4996 · 09-23 · 4 pages · FFS form
The official FFS 4-page fact-find form. Captures financial goals, customer data (full KYC), savings and investments, income/expenses, and a 6-question risk tolerance assessment producing a Conservative/Moderate/Aggressive score plus an Investor Profile model (1–5).
Supabase schemaAdd a customer_profiles table capturing: risk_tolerance_score, investor_profile (1–5), time_horizon, total_savings, household_income, tax_bracket, investment_experience.
Scoring engineRisk tolerance score feeds product suitability filtering. Conservative profile → FIAs and whole life. Aggressive → VAs and VULs. This prevents the AI from flagging an aggressive product for a conservative customer.
Meeting prepWhen the briefing surfaces a retirement appointment, pull the customer's profile score and display it: "Carlos Vega — Risk Score: Moderate (16 pts) — Investor Profile 3 — 10+ year horizon." You walk in prepared.
Asset snapshot

31-8352 · 04-19 · 1 page · Confidential
Calculates total assets at risk: real estate equity, potential wage garnishment, personal property, investment/retirement assets, and existing life insurance. Designed to show customers why they need protection — the gap between what they have and what they could lose.
Meeting toolUse before every life insurance and liability conversation. The "Total Assets at Risk" number creates urgency that raw premium numbers don't.
Digital formBuild as an interactive GHL survey or a standalone HTML calculator. Customer fills it out before the meeting — their total assets at risk pre-calculates and appears in your briefing before the appointment.
Scoring signalHigh total assets at risk + no permanent life insurance = maximum life cross-sell score. Add "assets_at_risk" field to Supabase customer_profiles table and weight it in the life score SQL.

Detailed income vs. expenses worksheet. Covers household essential, transportation, living, family care, medical/health, taxes, and discretionary categories. Identifies surplus cash flow available for investment or insurance premium.
