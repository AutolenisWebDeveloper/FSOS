Compliance boundaries
What AI and automation can do vs. what is legally reserved for you. Non-negotiable.
What AI agents CAN do
Answer inbound calls and route them
Book appointments on your calendar
Send educational content about products
Qualify interest and gather basic info
Send timed follow-up reminders
Reactivate dormant contacts
Answer FAQs from the knowledge base
Capture and log consent / opt-outs
Disclose AI identity at every interaction
Score and prioritize the book of business
What AI agents CANNOT do
Make any product recommendation
Conduct suitability determinations
Discuss surrender charges or tax treatment
Recommend an annuity or IRA rollover
Compare or select between products
Submit or process any application
Provide personalized financial advice
Close a life or financial services sale
Operate without an AI disclosure
Contact opted-out / DNC contacts

Regulatory framework
TCPA + FCC 24-17: AI voice = artificial voice, written consent required for marketing calls
Texas SB 140 (eff. 9/1/25): texts and automated messages covered, DTPA private right of action
TRAIGA (eff. 1/1/26): AI disclosure required, NIST AI RMF safe harbor, $10k–$200k penalties
FINRA Rule 2330 + Reg BI: any annuity/securities recommendation triggers suitability + best-interest obligations
Texas Ins. Code Ch. 1115: annuity best-interest + 4-credit training required
Farmers agent terms: APEX data = Farmers trade secret, no third-party scraping
The hard wall
Every workflow, every AI agent script, and every GHL sequence must be designed so that the moment a conversation moves from education or scheduling into recommendation, advice, suitability, or product selection — it immediately hands off to you. There is no exception. Farmers Financial Solutions is a FINRA-registered broker-dealer. You are the licensed professional. The AI's job is to get the right person in front of you at the right time. Your job is everything that happens in that meeting.
Your 3 required tasks
This is your entire operational role once the system is built. Everything else is automated.
1
Weekly · delegatable to VA
APEX book-of-business pull
Log into APEX, open your saved BOB report view, export CSV, drop in Google Drive folder. One agency takes under 5 minutes. Or hire a VA at $8–12/hr to do it under their own authorized login. Everything downstream fires automatically the moment the file lands.
45–75 min/week total · or $0 with VA
2
Daily · 10 minutes
Read your 7AM briefing
Every morning the system emails you a ranked priority list: new high-score opportunities, conversion deadlines, appointments booked, pipeline movements, opt-outs. You never log into APEX, AgencyZoom, or GHL to figure out what to do. The briefing tells you. Your job is to execute the top 5–10 actions.
10 min/day · no login required
3
Daily · your core value
Run licensed FSA meetings
Every OPRA review, term conversion, life needs analysis, and retirement discovery meeting was identified, outreached, prequalified, and booked by the system. You show up. You advise. You determine suitability. You make the recommendation. You submit the application. This is the work only a licensed FSA can do — and it's 100% of your time.
High-value · non-delegatable
